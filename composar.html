Human echolocation isn’t widely known across the visually impaired and blind community. While a group of researchers has shown that human echolocation is a learnable skill, several factors may contribute to its limited awareness and adoption. Not having a trainer to teach human echolocation, not knowing where to start, or not even knowing that it exists might be the reasons for this.


"Unconfined" is an app that teaches the basics of Human Echolocation to enhance daily navigation for visually impaired individuals. This technique suggests that objects in the real world resonate with sound, and by observing that sound and its reverberation, humans can determine whether the object is close or far. It incorporates ARKit, SpriteKit, and CoreML to deliver an experience that simulates objects and their associated feedback sounds when users make a tongue clicking sound typically used for echolocation. This app also supports VoiceOver to assist visually impaired individuals in navigating within the app. All resources used to create this app were entirely made using Logic Pro and Procreate.
